2021 EDITION

Instructions

  • Cryptowars2021 takes place in October 2021, from 25 to 30.
  • Registration deadline: 23 October 2021.
  • Start of the competition: 25 October 2021, 14:00 (Milan).
  • End of the competition: 30 October 2021, 23:59 (Milan).
  • Teams of a maximum of 5 students are admitted to the competition. With "students" we mean students enrolled in: undergraduate, three-year degree, master's degree and recent graduates (in 2021).
  • The participating team must be identified with a single email address. For subscribe you must send an email to cryptowars_decifris@di.unimi.it, having as subject the word REGISTRATION. The following must appear in the e-mail message:
    • the name of the team and the only reference e-mail address of the same,
    • the name of each individual participant, his / her university of origin of the individuals, his e-mail address.
    ATTENTION: all team members must be subscribed to our mailing list before sending the message.
  • Solutions must be sent to the same address used for enrollment in the format specified in the challenge file. Usually in .TXT format.
  • From 25 to 30 October, CRYPTOWARS participants can send just one solution to the same question.
  • Each question is associated with 1 point. The various teams acquire points in a manner incremental by solving the challenges and submitting the correct answers. If at the end of the race more teams have obtained the same score, checking the solution delivered via email, the winner is the one who completed the challenges in less time.

FAQ

  • Do I have to personally implement some code to solve the questions?
    It is not required to send the used code, so an easy way is to look for some code already implemented. The fact remains that, as the race has above all meant to lead to deep understanding of cryptographic problems, it is advised to work independently on their own code and its optimization.
  • Do I need to think about and implement new attacks?
    Not necessarily. There are many known attacks in the literature, so look for one that it seems easy to understand, implement it and get it running!
