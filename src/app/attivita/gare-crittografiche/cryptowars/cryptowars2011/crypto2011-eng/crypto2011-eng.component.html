2011 EDITION

  • CryptoWars2011 are an initiative of the Laboratory of Industrial Mathematics and Cryptography (Univ. Di Trento) with an informative purpose.
  • Individual enthusiasts or teams collide in the period October 2011 - February 2012 creating codes and breaking the codes of others! Teams of college students are especially welcome.
  • There are three categories, of increasing difficulty.

The initiative is presented during special meetings, called CryptoWarsCamp, which are important for organizing teams!

Competition description

In order to spread and arouse interest in Cryptography, we organize CryptoWars2011, which is a nationwide cryptography competition.

The timing
  • 12 September 2011: start of registrations of competitors, formal presentation of the tender during the Workshop BunnyTN.
  • 20 October 2011: registration deadline.
  • November 14, 2011: start of the competition.
  • 10 March 2012: end of the competition.
  • 12 March 2012: awarding of the winners during the Workshop BunnyTN.
Categories

The competition is divided into three categories. Competitors, who can be teams or individuals, can participate in one or more of the three categories.

  • Classic category.
    This is based on classical algorithms (in the sense of pre-Shannon), that is polyalphabetic, which in theory can be broken even with paper and pencil and good intuition. Each competitor must choose a system and encrypt a text provided by us, after which he must break the figures of the others! This category is also open to high school students (poster).
  • Public key category.
    We propose a series of public keys to be broken, of increasing difficulty and therefore with different scores. Mostly RSA, but also a few other systems. As a key is broken, points are awarded and the key is withdrawn.
  • Symmetric key category.
    We propose a symmetric system to attack. This category is very technical. There can be a lot of attack scenarios here, so we don't give a specific target, but we reward what seems like the most significant attack. We are more than happy to reward a couple of them too!
Award ceremony
  • The winners is awarded during a Workshop that we have in March 2012.
  • Each winning team, one per category (unless there is a tie), receive 5 licensed copies of the PrivateWave company software for making encrypted phone calls.
  • Each winning team also receive 3 iTwin keys to allow two computers to communicate securely.
  • On March 12, 2012 at the BunnyTN Workshop a person representing the winning team is awarded (one for each category). The prize consists of accommodation and food during the Workshop, plus of course the immortal glory (the Workshop and the award ceremony is streamed).
