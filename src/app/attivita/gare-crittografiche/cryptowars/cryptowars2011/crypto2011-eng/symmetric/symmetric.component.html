Symmetric key category

Rules

The symmetric cryptosystem that will be considered is BunnyTN. The algorithm specifications will be made available on the website from November 14th. The goal of each team is to find the most effective and fastest possible attack on BunnyTN. All types of attacks and scenarios are welcome. Some examples of attacks that can be found in the literature are the following:

  • Key-recovery attacks: attacks that try to reconstruct the secret key of the algorithm (the simplest example is to do an exhaustive search of all possible keys).
  • Distinguishing attacks: attacks that try to distinguish encrypted information from random messages.

Depending on the type of information the attacker has, different scenarios can be defined:

  • Related-key attacks: the attacker can observe the behavior of the cryptosystem with different secret keys not known but closely linked by mathematical relations (for example the first 80 bits of the keys are always the same)
  • Chosen-plaintext attacks: the attacker has the possibility to choose clear messages and to know the corresponding ciphertext.
  • Known-plaintext/ciphertext attacks: the attacker has random pairs of plaintext/ciphertext at his disposal (but which he cannot choose).
  • Chosen-ciphertext attacks: the attacker has the possibility to choose encrypted messages and to know the corresponding plaintext.
Score

The best attacks or attack attempts on the cryptosystem will be rewarded. With the same attack strategy, the one who managed to break the greatest number of rounds of the algorithm will be rewarded.
