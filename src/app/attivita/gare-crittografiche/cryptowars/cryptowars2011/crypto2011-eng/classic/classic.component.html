Classic category

Rules

This competition consists of 3 phases: a phase of preparation of the encryption algorithm, a phase of encryption of the message in clear text and a real competitive phase. In the first phase, each team has to decide which algorithm to use. The algorithm must respect certain characteristics and properties that are described here and must be sent to the e-mail address mathnow.unitn@gmail.com by October 20, 2011. At this point each team is sent the plaintext to be encrypted using their own algorithm. The plain text shows only the symbols present in the alphabet, the accents are replaced by the apostrophe, there are no numbers and it is all in lowercase characters. However, all texts is related to the same topic, which is announced on the site. Once the text has been encrypted by the team, it must be sent to mathnow.unitn@gmail.com by October 31, 2011. The last phase of the competition takes place from 31 October 2011 to 12 March 2012: each team must try to reconstruct the cipher text of the other teams, which is available on the site.

Submission

By 20 October 2011 each team must send the email mathnow.unitn@gmail.com the characteristics of its algorithm:

  • Group name (completely free);
  • the 5 permutations of the alphabet used;
  • the groups of letters corresponding to numbers 1-9;
  • the meaning of the number 0;

At this point the organizers send the plain text to each team. By October 31, 2011, each team must send their own ciphertext.

Score

The attack phase of the various algorithms begins on October 31, 2011. The encrypted texts of all participating teams is made available on the site and must be decrypted. Whenever a code is "broken", the team must report it to mathnow.unitn@gmail.com by sending the decrypted text. This code is then removed from the list of those still possible to attack. Simple "challenges" are also available on the site. Each team starts with an initial score of 20 points. When Team X manages to decrypt Team Y's ciphertext, X gains 10 points, Y loses 20. Each team can solve only one "challenge" by obtaining from 2 to 9 points. The winner is the team with the highest score. In the event of a tie, the team that first reached this score is considered the winner.

Algorithm

Each team must create a polyalphabetic algorithm, possibly with variants, to encrypt a clear message. The alphabet to be used corresponds to the English one, with the addition of punctuation symbols:

a b c d e f g h i j k l m n o p q r s t u v w x y z (space) . , ; : ' ! ?

for a total of 34 symbols. Therefore the text to be encrypted contain only and exclusively these 34 symbols (without considering the distinction between upper and lower case letters). A permutation of the alphabet is a rearrangement of symbols.

Example (1)

A simple permutation is to shift the symbols of the alphabet one position to the left. The letter a corresponds to its next b, a b corresponds to c and so on.

a b c d e f g h i j k l m n o p q r s t u v w x y z (space) . , ; : ' ! ?
b c d e f g h i j k l m n o p q r s t u v w x y z (space) . , ; : ' ! ? a

Each team must choose 5 permutation of the alphabet (all permutations are allowed, in total 34 factorial). The text to be encrypted is divided into blocks of 5 symbols, called words. The first letter of each block is replaced using the first permutation, the second letter of the block is replaced using the second permutation, and so on.

Example (2)

The permutations number 1, 3 and 5 are the same and correspond to the shift to the left of a position of the alphabet:

a b c d e f g h i j k l m n o p q r s t u v w x y z (space) . , ; : ' ! ?
b c d e f g h i j k l m n o p q r s t u v w x y z (space) . , ; : ' ! ? a

The permutations number 2 and number 4 correspond instead to the identity (i.e. the permutation that leaves everything fixed). The plaintext to be encrypted is as follows:

BUONA FORTUNA A TUTTE LE SQUADRE CHE PARTECIPANO ALLA GARA

This text is divided into words of 5 letters each: BUONA|(space)FORT|UNA(space)A|(space)TUTT|E(space)LE(space)|SQUAD|RE(space)CH|E (space)PAR|TECIP|ANO(space)A|LLA(space)G|ARA. so that it becomes: CUPNB|.FPRU|VNB(space)B|.TVTU|F(space)ME(space)|TQVAE|SE.CI|F(space)QAS|UEDIQ| BNP(space)B|MLB(SPACE)H|BRB, We observe that the last block is not composed of 5 letters, as the plaintext does not have a length that is a multiple of 5.

We stress that in the plain text there are no numbers, any accents is replaced by the apostrophe symbol (') and there is no distinction between uppercase and lowercase letters. Each team is allowed to apply variations to the simple polyalphabetic using up to a maximum of 9 special symbols, which must be indicated with the numbers from 1 to 9. Groups of consecutive letters (can also be words) are replaced by one of these symbols. Within each word these special symbols are not replaced by the permutation and therefore are skipped (CI1AOU is a word even if it has 6 symbols, but only 5 are part of the permutation alphabet).

Example (3)

We decide that 1 corresponds to the group of letters AO. CIAO(space)A(space)TUTTI it is divided into words by replacing AO with the number 1, but then not counting this symbol in the grouping: CI1(space)A(space)|TUTTI at this point the number 1 does not undergo any substitution during the encoding phase.

PRACTICAL CODE EXAMPLE (with variants)

Let's go back to the permutations mentioned in EXAMPLE (2). Let's define the special symbols:

  • 1 = GLI
  • 2 = RA
  • 3 = RE
  • 4 = "" (the empty string, not to be confused with the space)
  • 5 = CIAO COME STAI
  • 6 = PC
  • 7 = GN
  • 8 = ZZ
  • 9 = TT

Let's go back to the clear message of EXAMPLE (2):

"BUONA FORTUNA A TUTTE LE SQUADRE CHE PARTECIPANO ALLA GARA."

This text is divided into blocks taking into account the special symbols: BUONA|(space)FORT|UNA(space)A|(space)TU9E(space)|LE(space)SQ|UAD3(space)C| HE(space)PA|RTECI|PANO(space)|ALLA(space)|GA2. then encrypted it becomes: CUPNB|.FPRU|VNB(space)B|.TV9E.|ME.SR|VAE3(space)D|IE.PB|RTFCJ|QAOO.|BLMA.|HA2,

Each team also has the freedom to insert an additional special symbol, which must be indicated with the number 0, to represent an additional rule at will. For example, putting 0 in front of a word can simply indicate that this group of letters has been inverted (0ITTUT stands for ALL). The zero can be used up to a maximum of 10 times in the ciphertext. To avoid abuse, the particular use of zero must be approved by the organizers. However, in the last phase of the match (the attack on the coded texts of the various teams) the different possible meanings of this symbol is public (without saying which team uses that meaning).

ALGORITHM: Examples of legal and illegal 0s

Imagine being in a medieval tower at the service of your squire and having the task of encrypting and decrypting his messages. You have only your imagination and not the tools of abstract algebra. The zero must be used to indicate a simple rule, which can also be done by hand on a sheet of paper or in mind. Recall that with P1, P2, P3, P4 and P5 we indicate the 5 permutations used to encrypt the words.

Lawful use of the symbol 0
  • 0 acts only on an already encrypted word;
  • 0 can indicate the empty string;
  • (A1) given the XBCDE cipher, 0 can indicate that B is again replaced with a permutation (of those already used) depending on the letter that predicted it: for example, if X < Y I replace B with the permutation P1 otherwise with P2;
  • (A2) given the XYZTW cipher, 0 can indicate that the word is duplicated as follows 0XXYYZZTTWW (two words are obtained). X indicates X replaced with P1, Y indicates Y replaced with P2, Z indicates Z replaced with P3, T indicates T replaced with P4, W indicates W replaced with P5.
NOT lawful use of the symbol 0
  • Rule 0 should not be seen as a group letter operation. For example, it is not valid to use 0 to transform the word ABCDE into 0 (A + B) (A-B) CDE
