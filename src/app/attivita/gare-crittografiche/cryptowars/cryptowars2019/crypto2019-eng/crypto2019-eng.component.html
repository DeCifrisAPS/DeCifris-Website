Instructions

  • Cryptowars2019 takes place in 21--27 October 2019.
  • Team registration starts: 1 October 2019.
  • Registration deadline: 20 October 2019.
  • Competition: 21 October 2019, 14:00 (Milan).
  • End of the competition: 27 October 2019, 23:59 (Milan).
  • Teams of a maximum of 5 students are admitted to the competition. With "students" we mean students enrolled in: undergraduate, three-year degree, master's degree and PhD.
  • The participating team must be identified with a single email address. For subscribe you must send an email to cryptowarsdecifris2020@gmail.com, having as subject the word REGISTRATION. There must appear in the e-mail message the name of the participants, the University of origin of the individuals, the name of the team and the reference e-mail address.
  • From 21 to 27 October, CRYPTOWARS participants can send as many as THREE solutions to the same question. Only the last e-mail received is considered.
  • The solutions must be sent to cryptowars19@gmail.com in the format specified in the file of the challenge. Usually in .TXT format.
  • Here the file of the competition. In 2019 we asked questions of various kinds, which ranged from breaking some RSA keys to some specially designed ciphers, such as Makwa. We have also placed a few questions related to the world of elliptic curves.

FAQ

  • Do I have to personally implement some code to solve the questions?
    It is not required to send the used code, so an easy way is to look for some code already implemented. The fact remains that, as the race has above all meant to lead to deep understanding of cryptographic problems, it is advised to work independently on their own code and its optimization.
  • Do I need to think about and implement new attacks?
    Not necessarily. There are many known attacks in the literature, so look for one that it seems easy to understand, implement it and get it running!
