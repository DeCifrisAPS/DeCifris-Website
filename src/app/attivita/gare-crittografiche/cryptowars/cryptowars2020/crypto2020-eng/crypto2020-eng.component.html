2020 EDITION

Instructions

  • Cryptowars2020 takes place in October 2020, from 25 to 31.
  • Team registration starts: 19 October 2020.
  • Registration deadline: 24 October 2020.
  • Competition: 25 October 2020, 14:00 (Milan).
  • End of the competition: 31 October 2020, 23:59 (Milan).
  • Teams of a maximum of 5 students are admitted to the competition. With "students" we mean students enrolled in: undergraduate, three-year degree, master's degree and recent graduates (in 2020).
  • The participating team must be identified with a single email address. For subscribe you must send an email to cryptowarsdecifris2020@gmail.com, having as subject the word REGISTRATION. There must appear in the e-mail message the name of the participants, the University of origin of the individuals, the name of the team and the reference e-mail address.
  • From 25 to 31 October, CRYPTOWARS participants can send as many as THREE solutions to the same question. Only the last e-mail received will be considered.
  • Solutions must be sent to the same address used for enrollment in the format specified in the challenge file. Usually in .TXT format.
  • Each question is associated with 1 point. The various teams acquire points in a manner incremental by solving the challenges and submitting the correct answers. If at the end of the race more teams have obtained the same score, checking the last date and time solution delivered via email, the winner is the one who completed the challenges in less time.
  • The sponsors of the cryptographic race, through De Cifris, make available a prize of 10k Euros that are awarded as follows: 6k Euros to the team that will be classified in first place, 3k Euros to the second classified and finally 1k Euros to the third.

FAQ

  • Do I have to personally implement some code to solve the questions?
    It is not required to send the used code, so an easy way is to look for some code already implemented. The fact remains that, as the race has above all meant to lead to deep understanding of cryptographic problems, it is advised to work independently on their own code and its optimization.
  • Do I need to think about and implement new attacks?
    Not necessarily. There are many known attacks in the literature, so look for one that it seems easy to understand, implement it and get it running!
