11:30-13:30 Workshop

Andrea Bracciali, Davide Carboni, Francesca Medda
“CBDC's and tokenization of financial assets”

Invited speaker : TBA
Abstract : A significant transformation in the financial sector is the introduction of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs), which all the main central banks (ECB, BoE, BdI, …) are actively researching. While CBDCs offer potential efficiency benefits and innovation, they also raise concerns about centralization, control and citizen's privacy. Moreover, the tokenization of real-world (RWA) and financial assets also transforms and digitalise markets ranging from real estate to art, unlocking liquidity, and offering unprecedented opportunities for diversification and inclusion. Yet, these advancements are not without their challenges.
This workshop aims to stimulate discussions on CBDCs' and assets tokenization. The workshop includes an open discussion with expert panellists, intended to produce a policy briefing/position paper, and a series of speed talks on recent advances, to be collected in a post-workshop journal publication.
Detailed information and call for papers can be found on the “CBDC's and tokenization of financial assets” web page.

Room A
9:00-11:00 Workshop

Norberto Gavioli, Carmine Monetta, Marialaura Noce
“New trends in Group-Based Cryptography”

Invited speaker : TBA
Abstract : Group theory, and in particular non-abelian groups, offers a rich supply of complex and varied problems for cryptography. Indeed, group-based cryptography introduces a new dimension to the world of cryptography, offering challenging problems for both group theorists and cryptographers.
The aim of this workshop is to bring together specialists in the field of group based cryptography, where the speakers will present an overview of the current state-of-the-art in group-based cryptography. On the one hand they will describe protocols based on algorithmic problems in group theory, and, on the other hand, they will present families of groups that have been recently proposed as suitable platforms for cryptosystems. Their presentations have potential consequences both on cutting-edge cryptographic methodologies and on posing challenging mathematical questions that will captivate the minds of group theorists and cryptographers.
This workshop is designed to be a catalyst for collaboration, both on a national and international scale. and represents an opportunity to not only deepen our understanding of post-quantum group-based cryptography but also to cultivate a vibrant network for possible collaborations in the cryptographic research and applications.

11:30-13:30 Workshop

Silvio Ranise, Alessandro Tomasi, D’Intino Andrea, Roio Denis
“Topics in Applied Cryptography”

Invited speaker : TBA
Abstract : This workshop is dedicated to cryptography with a specific application, scenario and/or technology in mind, including but not limited to performance evaluation, implementation issues, and requirements for unusual application scenarios. Purely theoretical results are out of scope. Applications, protocols, and scenarios in scope include but are not limited to:

  • Privacy Enhancing Cryptography, e.g., selective disclosure signatures, accumulators, anonymous credentials, zero-knowledge proofs;
  • Partially or fully homomorphic encryption for the cloud;
  • Secret sharing and multi-party threshold schemes for decentralisation of trust;
  • Cryptography in protocols e.g., TLS, SSH, Double Ratchet, 3DH, OAuth, including contributions, security assessments, or implementations;
  • Software libraries implementing cryptography, APIs, and related issues;
  • Hardware providing cryptographic services, and related issues, e.g.
    • IoT;
    • SmartNICs;
    • Security controllers;
    • Cryptographic processors;
    • PUF;
    • Re-used, related, or weak keys and certificates;
    • Poor RNG;
  • Attacks and vulnerabilities: side-channel, fault injection, oracles, key recovery;
  • Quantum Key Distribution;
  • Cryptography in blockchain infrastructure and decentralized applications.

Room B
9:00-11:00 Workshop

Marco Pedicini, Ilaria Chillotti, Michela Iezzi
“Privacy-preserving Machine Learning Workshop”

Invited speaker : TBA
Abstract : Privacy-preserving machine learning is an increasingly vital field as organizations and individuals strive to leverage machine learning while safeguarding sensitive data. This workshop addresses the growing demand for privacy enhancing techniques. It offers an up-to-date overview of the key concepts, methods, and tools. Participants will be introduced to essential cryptographic techniques.
The workshop's structure consists of a keynote on Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) by Andrei Stoian, a Data Scientist at Zama's Concrete-ML group. Additionally, it features contributions from various experts, including PhD students, early-stage researchers, and industry professionals, focusing on topics such as:

  • Differential Privacy;
  • Federated Learning;
  • Fully Homomorphic Encryption;
  • Secure Multi-Party Computation;
  • Trusted Execution Environment.

The keynote delves into the practical aspects of FHE, discussing both advancements and challenges. Furthermore, the workshop invites contributions related to these topics, providing a platform for discussing real-world applications, enabling young researchers to showcase their work, and receive valuable insights and suggestions.

Marco Baldi, Giulio Codogni, Roberto La Scala, Alessio Meneghetti, Gerardo Pelosi, Federico Pintore
“Recent Advances in Post-Quantum Cryptography”

Invited speaker : TBA
Abstract : Post-quantum cryptography, that is, the study of cryptographic schemes capable of withstanding attackers equipped with both classical and quantum computers, is an area of research that has experienced tremendous growth in recent years, also due to the initiation of a standardization effort by NIST. Since last June, the focus has been on post-quantum digital signature schemes, which are the subject of the latest NIST competition.
This workshop will provide an overview of the state of the art in post-quantum cryptography, along with the NIST competition, and focus on cutting-edge methods for designing and implementing post-quantum.
